ISR-vote MVP: Minimum Viable Product

A Comprehensive Guide to How ISR-vote Works and Why It Matters

‚ñ∫ Introduction: What Happens When You Join

When you join an ISR community, you're not just voting ‚Äì you're participating in a system that calculates your influence based on three factors:

Your ISR (Index of Social Responsibility) Score = Your Relevance + Your Expertise + Your Contribution

This score changes for each decision based on how relevant you are to the specific issue. An expert plumber has more influence on roof repairs, but equal voice on common community issues.

The MVP application consists of 8 sections:

  1. Profile ‚Äì Your skills, experience, and interests
  2. Values ‚Äì Your preferences stated in a convenient way
  3. Actions ‚Äì Log of your contributions and activities
  4. Chat ‚Äì Discuss issues with relevant community members
  5. Voting ‚Äì Make decisions on problems and solutions
  6. Tasks ‚Äì Track implementation of approved decisions
  7. Settings ‚Äì UI preferences
  8. AI-assistant

üéØ User Success Tip: Start by completing your profile and values ‚Äì this allows the AI to identify decisions you're most relevant to and show you the most important discussions.

‚ñ∫ 1. Build Your Profile: Show What You Bring

Your profile is your identity in the system. It tells the community about your expertise, experience, and what makes you valuable to group decisions.

What Goes in Your Profile?

  • Professional Skills: Certifications, licenses, specialized knowledge (e.g., "Licensed Electrician", "Project Manager", "Accountant")
  • Experience: Relevant projects you've completed or managed (e.g., "5 years managing building renovations")
  • Interests: What you care about and want to stay informed about (e.g., "Energy efficiency", "Community gardens", "Safety")
  • Certifications: Formal qualifications that validate expertise

üéØ UX Success Tips

  • Be specific: Instead of "IT skills", say "Firebase database administration" or "Full-stack web development"
  • Include verification: Add links to certificates, portfolios, or past projects when possible
  • Update regularly: As you complete tasks and gain experience, update your profile to reflect new expertise
  • Honest over impressive: Overstating skills is flagged by the system and decreases your ISR

‚ñ∫ 2. Define Your Values: What Matters to You

Microcommunity values are the foundation of ISR calculations. They define what behavior the community rewards and what it penalizes (as an addition to normal general law). Each microcommunity formed around rised issue gather its own Matrix of Values - out from all microcommunity members' values.

The Matrix of Values

Each microcommunity gathers a Matrix of Values ‚Äì a collective agreement on what's important out from all microcommunity members' values regarding personal Relevance. Vectorized Matrix of Values gives Vector of Values - pure mathematical representation which is used in Contribution calculations.

Basic ways to fill out values are:
  • Pick a popular tamplate from the list ("democrat", "buddist", "feminist", "greenpeace", etc.)
  • Create a characteristics list with some points disposed - just like in a computer game.
  • Pass a survay with AI assistant.
  • Write you own text explanation.
  • Combine options above.

How to Define Values

Option A: Manual Definition

  1. Open Values section in profile
  2. Click "Add Value"
  3. Type the value name and description
  4. Mark points to the value

Option B: Narative explanation

  1. Open Values section in profile
  2. Click "Narative"
  3. Express your beliefs and life orienteers

Option C: AI-Assisted Definition (Recommended for First-Time Setup)

  1. Click "AI Guide Me" button
  2. AI asks 10-15 questions about community priorities
  3. Based on your answers, AI proposes a values structure
  4. You adjust points and descriptions as needed
  5. Community reviews and collectively refines

AI Assistant Feature

After you define a value, you can ask the AI:

  • "What do we mean by 'technical competence'?" ‚Äì AI clarifies the concept
  • "Give me examples of this value in action" ‚Äì AI provides concrete scenarios
  • "How does this compare to other microcommunity members?" ‚Äì AI shows benchmarks
  • "Explain my values list in a narrative view" ‚Äì AI makes a story about your values with nearest political examples

üéØ UX Success Tips

  • Make values specific and measurable: Not "trust", but "transparent financial reporting" or "responds within 24 hours"
  • Be honest and aware: Values should reflect your individual preferences
  • Revisit quarterly: As you and reality around change, update your values.
  • Do not change your values dramatically: The system is setted to recognize suspicious activity like when a user changes all their values at once to manipulate ISR.

‚ñ∫ 3. Join Discussions: Microcommunities Around Issues

The Chat section is where collective problem-solving starts. When an issue arises, the AI identifies the most relevant members (a "microcommunity") and notifies them.

How Discussions Work

  1. Someone creates a topic: "Roof needs repairs ‚Äì what should we do?" specifying exact place, size of the problem, time to the problem should be solved and other context data
  2. AI identifies microcommunity: Finds members skilled in construction, interested in maintenance, affected by the issue
  3. Relevant members are notified: Get badge with ISR relevance score for this topic
  4. Discussion unfolds: Members share expertise, ask questions, propose solutions
  5. Evidence uploaded: Photos, quotes, technical documents attached to messages

Microcommunity Formation (How AI Selects Relevant Members)

When a topic is created, the system calculates relevance using:

  • Profile match: Keywords in topic match skills in profile (e.g., "plumbing" matches "Licensed Plumber")
  • Interest alignment: Member marked this category as interest
  • Past participation: Member has voted or commented on similar topics before
  • Geographic proximity: If relevant (e.g., "Entrance 3" water issue ‚Üí notify Entrance 3 residents)
  • Values alignment: Topic involves value member cares about

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: You can ALWAYS find and join any discussion, even if you're not in the AI-selected microcommunity. The system recommends but doesn't restrict participation.

üéØ UX Success Tips

  • Read before posting: Check if your point has already been made or discussed. Remember, any issue publishing starts microcommunity gathering and ISR calculation which is costly and spend society resoures. So false, dubbling, dumb issues could be assessed negatively and decrease your future ISR.
  • Use evidence: Attach photos, quotes, or documents to support your position
  • Stay on topic: Emoji reactions showing irrelevance or disrespect will be tracked in your ISR
  • Ask questions respectfully: Challenge ideas, not people
  • Respond to feedback: If flagged as irrelevant, explain your reasoning - shows intellectual integrity

‚ñ∫ 4. Participate in Decisions: Your Vote Has Weight

The Voting section is where discussion becomes decision. Each voting topic has multiple options, and your vote's weight depends on your ISR for that specific issue.

The Voting Process

  1. Topic creator posts options with descriptions (cost, timeline, pros/cons)
  2. Discussion phase: Community shares evidence and expertise (optional)
  3. System calculates your ISR for this specific decision based on:
    • Your relevance to the issue (matches to the issue affection)
    • Your expertise level (matches to decision domain)
    • Your track record (matches to current Matrix of Values)
  4. You vote - your click carries your calculated weight
  5. System tallies weighted votes - highest weight option wins
  6. Status changes to "closed" and implementation begins

The mechanism ISR calculation for Voting section is sligtly different then for Chat. In Chat we search for variants how to solve the problem, so Expertise gets sligtly havier wight over Relevance and the gathered microcommunity increasing because of those invided experts. In Voting people who gets affect from the issue make the decision out of considered options, so Relevance is havier than Expertise and relevant only can vote.

Example: Roof Repair Voting

You are a licensed roofer. The building needs $50K in repairs. Three options are proposed.

Your ISR for this decision: 1.93

  • Relevance (R): 1.0 (you are a dweller of the building)
  • Expertise (E): 4.0 (licensed roofer - perfect match)
  • Contribution (C): 1.1 (you consistently prioritize quality and safety)
  • Final weight: 0.4√ó1.0 + 0.3√ó4.0 + 0.3√ó1.1 = your ISR of final vote

Your vote carries 1.93 the weight of an average non-expert member with neutral track record (who votes with 1.0 weight).

Understanding Your ISR Score: Why It Changes Per Decision

  • Same person, different decisions = different weights
    • You're a roofer ‚Üí high weight on roof decisions
    • Same decision on landscaping ‚Üí low weight (not your expertise)
  • Community feedback updates contribution: After decisions are implemented, ratings from community update your activity score

üéØ UX Success Tips

  • Click "Why My Weight?" to understand your ISR - transparency builds trust
  • State your opinion only on issues you understand: Flooding discussion make decision-making harder
  • Check the discussion first: Read expert opinions before voting
  • Don't wait till deadline: Vote early - shows you're engaged

‚ñ∫ 5. Track Implementation: From Decision to Reality

The Tasks section brings voting decisions to life. After a vote concludes, the winning option becomes a task that tracks progress and completion.

How Tasks Work

  1. Vote concludes ‚Üí system automatically creates a task from the winning option
  2. Task properties: Deadline, budget, responsible person, sub-tasks (if broken down)
  3. Status updates: Progress tracked: "Open" ‚Üí "In Progress" ‚Üí "Completed" ‚Üí "Evaluated"
  4. Community rates outcome: After completion, members review the task and assess its matters

ISR Calculation Pipeline

  1. Trigger: Rised chat or vote issue
  2. Data Fetch: Get users profile, values, issue context, historical performance
  3. Calculate Relevance (R):
    • Extract keywords/domain from topic (Vertex AI NLP)
    • For each community member, calculate Relevance (R):
      • keyword_match (how well profile matches topic)
      • interest_tags (does member follow this category)
      • past_engagement (participated in similar topics before)
      • geographic_relevance (if applicable)
      • values_alignment (does member prioritize this value type)
  4. Microcommunity Formation:
    • Filter: users with R > threshold gather Microcommunity
  5. Calculate Expertise (E):
    • Match user skills to decision domain (using Vertex AI semantic analysis)
    • Check interest keywords in user profile
    • Look at past performance in similar domains
    • Result: Multiplier 0.0√ó ‚Äì 10.0√ó
  6. Matrix of Values & Vector of Values evaluation:
    • Matrix of Values: gathering of Microcommunity members values
    • Vector of Values: define from the vector representations of the microcommunity members values, taking into account Relevance (R)
  7. Calculate Contribution (C):
    • Analyze user's communication (NLP: tone, respectfulness, on-topic)
    • Check alignment with community values (via Vertex AI content analysis)
    • Count positive vs negative emoji reactions from community
    • Count contribution history (posts, votes, task participation)
    • Analyze outcomes: past votes that led to good implementations
    • Track community feedback: ratings on completed tasks
    • Result: Multiplier 0.0√ó ‚Äì 10.0√ó
  8. ISR components weights (wR, wE, wC) evaluation: AI analize issue and its context to set wieghts for Relevanse, Expertise and Contribution parts
  9. Apply Formula: Row ISR = wR√óR + wE√óE + wC√óC
  10. Normalization: Individual ISR is normalized to a microcommunity average of 1.0
  11. Store & Notify: Save ISR for later use, notify user with microcommunity invitation and their personal weight on the issue

Performance Optimizations

  • ISR Caching: Recalculated once per decision, cached for voting period
  • Lazy Loading: Load messages/tasks in batches as you scroll
  • Cloud Function Scaling: Auto-scales to handle N simultaneous ISR calculations
  • CDN Delivery: Static assets (UI, styles) served from edge locations
  • Database Indexing: Optimized queries for fast searches and sorting

Privacy & Security (Adaptable Authorization)

  • Multi-Layer Data Access: What data a members can see about other members depends on their gISR (general ISR) and Person-to-Person graph connections.
  • Access Logging: Every data request logged and auditable
  • Data Misuse Detection: AI flags suspicious access patterns (excessive requests = ISR penalty)
  • Encryption: All data encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest
